# Getting Started with Electric Vehicles

## Is an EV Right for You?

Before purchasing an electric vehicle, consider the following:

- **Daily driving range**: Most EVs offer 150–300+ miles of range per charge. If your daily commute is under 50 miles, almost any EV will work well.
- **Home charging**: Having access to a garage or dedicated parking spot makes home charging easy. Installing a Level 2 charger at home is highly recommended.
- **Public charging network**: Check the availability of charging stations in your area using apps like PlugShare or the ChargePoint app.

## Types of Electric Vehicles

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **BEV** (Battery Electric Vehicle) | Runs entirely on electricity. No gas engine. Examples: Tesla Model 3, Chevy Bolt, Nissan Leaf |
| **PHEV** (Plug-in Hybrid) | Has both an electric motor and a gas engine. Can be plugged in. Examples: Toyota Prius Prime, Ford Escape PHEV |
| **HEV** (Hybrid Electric Vehicle) | Combines gas engine with electric assist, but cannot be plugged in. Examples: Toyota Prius, Honda Accord Hybrid |

## What to Look for When Buying

1. **Range** — How many miles can it travel on a full charge?
2. **Charging speed** — What is the maximum AC and DC fast-charge rate (in kW)?
3. **Battery warranty** — Most manufacturers offer 8 years / 100,000 miles on the battery.
4. **Incentives** — Federal tax credits up to $7,500 may be available (see [Cost & Savings](cost-and-savings.md)).
5. **Total cost of ownership** — EVs typically have lower fuel and maintenance costs.

## Test Drive Tips

- Pay attention to one-pedal driving (regenerative braking).
- Test highway range and acceleration.
- Explore the infotainment and charging software.
