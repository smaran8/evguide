# EV Charging Guide

## Charging Levels

| Level | Voltage | Speed | Best For |
|-------|---------|-------|----------|
| **Level 1** | 120V (standard outlet) | ~3–5 miles per hour | Overnight charging for short commuters |
| **Level 2** | 240V (dedicated circuit) | ~15–30 miles per hour | Home charging, workplaces, public lots |
| **DC Fast Charge (Level 3)** | 480V+ | ~100–250+ miles in 20–30 min | Road trips, quick top-ups |

## Home Charging

Installing a **Level 2 EVSE (Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment)** at home is the most convenient way to keep your EV charged.

### Steps to Set Up Home Charging

1. **Choose an EVSE unit** — Popular brands include ChargePoint, JuiceBox, and Emporia.
2. **Hire a licensed electrician** — A dedicated 240V / 50A circuit is required for most Level 2 chargers.
3. **Check for rebates** — Many utilities offer rebates for home charger installation ($100–$500+).
4. **Schedule off-peak charging** — Set your car to charge during off-peak hours (usually overnight) to save on electricity costs.

### Estimated Home Charging Cost

- Average US electricity rate: ~$0.13/kWh
- A 60 kWh battery costs roughly **$7.80** to fully charge at home
- Equivalent to driving ~250 miles for under $8

## Public Charging

### Finding Stations

- **PlugShare** — Community-sourced map of all public chargers
- **ChargePoint** — Large US network of Level 2 and fast chargers
- **Electrify America** — Nationwide DC fast charging (great for road trips)
- **Tesla Supercharger** — Tesla's proprietary network (now open to other brands at many locations)
- Built-in navigation in most EVs can route you to chargers automatically

### Tips for Public Charging

- Always check the connector type your car supports (CCS, CHAdeMO, NACS/Tesla).
- Charge to 80% on DC fast chargers — charging slows significantly above 80%.
- Avoid letting the battery drop below 10% regularly.

## Connector Types

| Connector | Used By |
|-----------|---------|
| **J1772** | All US EVs for Level 1 & Level 2 AC charging |
| **CCS (Combined Charging System)** | Most non-Tesla EVs for DC fast charging |
| **CHAdeMO** | Older Nissan Leaf, Mitsubishi Outlander PHEV |
| **NACS (Tesla connector)** | Tesla vehicles; now being adopted by Ford, GM, and others |
