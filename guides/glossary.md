# EV Glossary

A quick reference for common electric vehicle terms and acronyms.

---

**BEV** — Battery Electric Vehicle. A vehicle powered entirely by an electric motor and battery pack, with no internal combustion engine.

**CHAdeMO** — A DC fast-charging connector standard developed in Japan. Used mainly by older Nissan Leaf and Mitsubishi models.

**CCS** — Combined Charging System. A DC fast-charging connector standard widely used by non-Tesla EVs in North America and Europe.

**DCFC** — DC Fast Charging. A high-power charging method (Level 3) capable of adding 100+ miles of range in under 30 minutes.

**EVSE** — Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment. The hardware used to charge an EV, commonly called a "charger."

**HEV** — Hybrid Electric Vehicle. A vehicle with both a gasoline engine and an electric motor, but it cannot be plugged in (the battery charges via regenerative braking).

**J1772** — The standard AC charging connector used in North America for Level 1 and Level 2 charging. Compatible with all US EVs.

**kW** — Kilowatt. A measure of power. Used to describe the charging rate of an EVSE (e.g., 7.2 kW Level 2 charger) or an EV's motor output.

**kWh** — Kilowatt-hour. A measure of energy. Used to describe battery capacity (e.g., 82 kWh battery) and the amount of energy consumed while driving.

**NACS** — North American Charging Standard. Tesla's charging connector, now being adopted by Ford, GM, and other automakers as the new industry standard.

**One-Pedal Driving** — A driving mode where lifting off the accelerator applies strong regenerative braking, allowing the driver to slow and stop the car without using the brake pedal.

**PHEV** — Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle. A vehicle with both a gasoline engine and a rechargeable battery that can be plugged in. Typically offers 20–50 miles of all-electric range.

**Range** — The distance an EV can travel on a single full charge, typically measured in miles or kilometers.

**Range Anxiety** — The fear of running out of charge before reaching a destination or charging station.

**Regenerative Braking** — A system that captures kinetic energy during deceleration and converts it back into electrical energy to recharge the battery.

**SOC** — State of Charge. The current battery level expressed as a percentage (e.g., 80% SOC).

**Supercharger** — Tesla's proprietary DC fast-charging network. Many Supercharger stations are now open to non-Tesla vehicles.

**TOU** — Time of Use. A utility pricing structure where electricity costs vary by time of day. Charging during off-peak hours (often overnight) can significantly reduce costs.

**V2G** — Vehicle-to-Grid. Technology that allows an EV to send stored electricity back to the power grid, potentially earning credits from the utility.

**V2H** — Vehicle-to-Home. Technology that allows an EV to power a home during an outage using its battery.
